# Customer-Segmentation-for-Marketing-Strategy

Problem

Businesses often apply uniform marketing strategies across their customer base, leading to inefficient spending and missed opportunities. This project aims to segment customers into meaningful groups to support targeted marketing and improved customer engagement.

Approach

The analysis followed a structured workflow:

Data selection and preprocessing of customer attributes (income and spending behaviour)
Feature scaling to ensure fair clustering performance
Identification of optimal cluster size using the Elbow Method and Silhouette Score
Application of K-Means clustering to segment customers
Interpretation of clusters based on behavioural patterns
Results

The model identified distinct customer segments based on income and spending behaviour. Each segment represents a unique group with different characteristics, enabling more targeted business strategies.

Business Impact

The segmentation enables businesses to:

Identify high-value customers for retention strategies
Target specific groups with personalised marketing campaigns
Optimise marketing spend by focusing on profitable segments
Improve customer engagement and long-term value
Key Skills Demonstrated
Clustering and unsupervised learning (K-Means)
Feature scaling and preprocessing
Model evaluation using silhouette score
Data visualisation and interpretation
Translating analytical results into business insights
